FAQ

Why have I chosen to do an apprenticeship?

The traditional university path certainly has its benefits but I am of the belief that apprenticeships, particularly in the tech industry, can prove a superior route as you are given the opportunity to study and work simultaneously amongst professionals. With coding, I find that the best way to learn is through practice. Theory is crucial but application is invaluable. I hope to reap the benefits of both by completing a degree apprentiship.

What can I contribute to the firm?

My dedication and persistence. I am committed to my studies and my career. When I am met with a task, I do everything within my power to complete it to the highest of standards and continue learning and progressing as I go. An example of this is this website. I am coding this it using HTML, CSS and JavaScript just two months into beginning coding- just in time for my apprenticeship applications. I work well in a team setting and am confident I can bring a fresh and varied perspective to the firm.

What are my goals?

I want to use this apprenticeship as a foundation for my career in the tech industry. For the course of the apprenticeship I hope to pick up the relevant skills and connections that will let me progress in this field. I would be glad to have the opportunity to continue as a permanent employee at your firm. Besides work I also want to make a difference to my homeland- perhaps I will be able to do so with the use of modern technology.

What is my greatest achievement?

Halfway through Year 8 in secondary school, I began studying Russian independently. In Year 10, I sat the GCSE and got a grade 9 (A**). I have now achieved an A* in the Russian A-level. Next, I decided to improve my native Persian. Through reading and contributing to blogs and following the works of more affluent speakers and writers, I can now proudly say that people often tell me my Persian sounds academic! I have a deep interest in expanding my knowledge of different languages and have now moved onto learning Spanish.
